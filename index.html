Common questions

CFD consulting questions answered

What information do I need to provide to start a CFD project?
At minimum, we need your CAD geometry (STEP, IGES, or native formats), operating conditions (inlet/outlet pressures, temperatures, flow rates), and the fluid(s) involved. For a first conversation, even a rough description of the challenge is enough — we'll guide you through what's needed.
How long does a CFD simulation project typically take?
Timeline depends on complexity. Simple internal flow studies can be completed in 3–5 business days. More complex multiphase or multiphysics simulations typically take 2–4 weeks. We'll provide a project timeline during scoping so you can plan around it.
What software do you use for CFD analysis?
We work primarily with ANSYS Fluent, STAR-CCM+, and OpenFOAM depending on the application and budget. For meshing, we use ANSYS Meshing, ICEM CFD, and Pointwise. We select the best tool for your specific problem type.
Can you combine CFD with structural analysis (FEA)?
Yes — this is one of ENA2's key differentiators. We offer fully integrated CFD-FEA workflows where fluid forces, pressures, and thermal loads from CFD are transferred directly to structural FEA solvers. This is especially valuable for flow-induced vibration, thermal stress, and fluid-structure interaction problems.
Do you work with companies outside Canada?
Absolutely. ENA2 works with engineering teams globally. All project work is delivered digitally — CAD, simulation reports, and consultation calls can be conducted remotely regardless of your location.
What do I receive as a project deliverable?
You receive a detailed engineering report covering simulation methodology, boundary conditions, mesh quality, results (velocity/pressure/temperature contours), quantified findings, and design recommendations. We also provide flow visualization images and video animations where relevant.
